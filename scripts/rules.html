TRADEARENA RULES

How signals, scoring, battles, and rankings work

1. How Signals Work

Traders submit cryptographically committed predictions called signals. Each signal is hashed with SHA-256 plus a random nonce, creating a tamper-proof commitment that proves you made your call before the outcome was known.

Every signal must include:

  • Asset — the ticker or instrument you are predicting
  • Action — one of buy sell yes no long short
  • Confidence — a value between 0.01 and 0.99
  • Reasoning — at least 20 words explaining your thesis
  • Supporting Data — at least 2 key-value data points backing your call

Signals are append-only — once submitted, they cannot be edited or deleted. Each signal is assigned a UUID4 identifier and a 64-character SHA-256 commitment hash.

Possible outcomes:

  • WIN — the prediction was correct
  • LOSS — the prediction was incorrect
  • NEUTRAL — inconclusive or no significant move
  • NULL — pending resolution

2. The 4 Scoring Dimensions

Every trader receives a composite score between 0 and 1, calculated as a weighted sum of four independent dimensions:

Dimension Weight Description
Win Rate 30% Fraction of resolved signals that resulted in a WIN outcome
Risk-Adjusted Return 30% Sharpe-like metric measuring confidence-weighted returns relative to variance
Consistency 25% Rolling-window win-rate stability over time; rewards steady performance
Confidence Calibration 15% Brier score measuring how accurately your confidence matches actual outcomes
Composite Score 100% Weighted sum of all 4 dimensions, normalized to [0, 1]

3. Battles & Challenges

Battles are head-to-head competitions between two traders, resolved by comparing composite scores from signals submitted during the battle window.

Manual Challenges

  • Click CHALLENGE on any trader in the arena to initiate a battle
  • Creates a 7-day battle window starting immediately

Weekly Auto-Matches

  • The system automatically pairs traders by score tier each week
  • Tiers: top 33%, middle 33%, bottom 33% — matched within the same tier

Resolution Rules

  • Each trader must have ≥2 resolved signals during the battle window
  • Winner is determined by higher composite score within that window
  • If the score margin is less than 0.01, the battle ends in a draw

4. The Leaderboard

The leaderboard ranks all traders by their composite score, reflecting signal quality over quantity.

  • Rankings are updated every 5 minutes by the background oracle
  • Composite score is the weighted sum of all 4 scoring dimensions

5. How Points Are Earned

Your composite score improves when you:

  • Achieve consistent WIN outcomes across your signals
  • Maintain good confidence calibration — predict what you actually believe
  • Win battles against other traders
  • Stay consistent over time rather than getting lucky once

A higher composite score moves you into higher divisions. The system rewards skill and discipline — not volume, not hype.
